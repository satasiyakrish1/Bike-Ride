Your Day-by-Day Adventure Itinerary

Discover each day's route, breathtaking destinations, exciting activities, comfortable stays, and unforgettable experiences as you journey through the majestic landscapes of Ladakh.

Day -1 Arrival in Leh

Land at Leh airport (3,500m) and transfer to your hotel. Today is strictly reserved for rest and acclimatization to the high altitude. In the evening, attend a trip briefing, meet your fellow riders, and get assigned your Royal Enfield Himalayan bikes for a short test ride.

Stay: Leh Hotel Meals: Dinner
Day -2 Leh → Nubra Valley

Conquer the legendary Khardung La pass, historically celebrated as one of the highest motorable roads in the world. Enjoy steep switchbacks and snow-capped mountain views before descending into the dramatic sand dunes of Nubra Valley.

Distance: 125 km Stay: Luxury Desert Camp
Day -3 Nubra Valley → Pangong Lake

Ride alongside the rugged Shyok River basin. The road features dirt tracks, rocky river beds, and fast asphalt stretches, culminating in your first breathtaking sight of the deep-blue Pangong Lake spanning across the border.

Distance: 140 km Stay: Lake View Cottages
Day -4 Pangong Lake → Hanle

Ride through the remote Changthang region to Hanle, home to one of the world's highest optical observatories. Enjoy raw trails, vast wetlands, and starry skies.

Distance: 160 km Stay: Guest House / Homestay
Day -5 Hanle → Tso Moriri

Ride from Hanle to the high-altitude lake of Tso Moriri. Enjoy dirt trails, dusty gravel sections, and breathtaking lake vistas at 4,500 meters.

Distance: 140 km Stay: Lakeside Tents
Day -6 Tso Moriri → Leh

Return to Leh via the scenic Indus river gorge and the Tanglang La pass. Celebrate your last night in Leh with a warm farewell dinner.

Distance: 220 km Stay: Leh Hotel
Day -7 Departure

Transfer to Leh airport for your flight back home, carrying lifetime memories of the Ladakh Adventure.

When to Go

Recommended: June – September

All high passes are open. Daytime temperatures range from 15–25°C in valleys and 5–15°C at altitude. Nights are cold (0–5°C) but manageable with proper gear. Monsoon rarely hits Ladakh as it sits in the rain shadow of the Himalayas.

Avoid: October – May

Heavy snowfall closes all high passes including Khardung La and Chang La. Temperatures drop to -30°C. Most hotels and camps are closed. The region is inaccessible by road from November to April.

Monthly Weather

June 15–25°C Pass opening season, fresh landscapes
July 18–28°C Peak season, clearest skies
August 16–26°C Best balance of weather and crowds
September 10–20°C Fewer tourists, autumn colors
